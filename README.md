# Constraint-First Contact Model (SEED v1.0)

This repository contains **SEED v1.0**, a speculative, constraint-based analytical framework for examining a *very narrow class* of anomalous observations using long-horizon, system-level reasoning.

The framework is explicitly **conditional**, **falsifiable**, and **non-anthropocentric**. It is designed to explain patterns of **restraint, ambiguity, and absence**, rather than presence, intent, or disclosure.

---

## What this is

- A conceptual framework, not a claim about reality
- A constraint-first model emphasizing limits, stability, and information containment
- Applicable only to a small subset of anomalous cases with specific characteristics
- Designed to be falsifiable and bounded in scope
- Focused on system-level behavior rather than narrative interpretation

---

## What this is not

- Not evidence of extraterrestrial or non-human presence
- Not an explanation for all UFO/UAP or anomalous phenomena
- Not a disclosure hypothesis
- Not a belief system, ideology, or narrative account
- Not a substitute for psychological, sociological, or technological explanations

---

## Core idea (brief)

SEED explores how a highly advanced, long-horizon system—if it existed—might interact with a developing civilization under strong constraints. In this framework, **constraints shape behavior more than intent**, leading to:

- Minimal or no communication  
- Strong information containment  
- Procedural, non-escalatory interaction  
- Tolerated but ambiguous visibility  
- Persistent lack of resolution  

Coherence within the framework does **not** imply truth.
